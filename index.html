  1. One question that arises from this data is why the United States spends so much more than other nations on healthcare. The answer is certainly not that we need to spend that money in order to get the superior results because we don’t. There are many factors, but among the dominant ideas is that healthcare is simply more expensive in the United States for similar kinds of services than it is in other countries. This analysis takes into consideration the differences in cost of living.

Page 16

  1. Another factor that contributes to the cost of healthcare, poor overall performance, and inequities in access and health, is the fact that in United States we use a lot more high-priced, sophisticated, technology driven systems, that produce benefits for only a small share of the population. We use less routine and prevention-oriented services than are used in other developed countries.

You can also see that the United States spends much less on what is described here as social care. United States is the only country in single digits on this chart in this area.

Page 18

  1. As we discussed previously, this notion of spending on social care is often described as addressing the “Social Determinants Of Health”. This chart, from the California Department of Public Health website, depicts the many factors that contribute to people being healthy or not. As you can see, they include social inequities, institutional inequities, living conditions, risk behaviors, exposure to disease and injuries, and contributors to mortality. There is strong evidence that these factors are strong contributors to both individual and population health.

Page 19

  1. However, as you saw a few slides back, in the United States most of our money goes to this one circled area on the chart, health care. There is increasing understanding that if we are to improve the performance of our health systems to levels enjoyed by the other developed countries in the world, we must shift some of our spending to address the social determinants of health.

Page 20

  1. In fact, in 2016 the Institute of Medicine published this “Framework for Educating Health Professionals to Address the Social Determinants of Health.” They made the point that, across all health professions, health professions students need to begin their professional careers with a better understanding of the social determinants of health. They also make the point that even in care systems that are focused on individual health, providers who understand the broader context of social determinants can do a better job delivering individual care. And in addition, providers who understand the concepts involved in the social determinants of health can influence healthcare systems to produce better results by addressing social determinants.

Page 21

  1. Many other groups have documented similar findings. This 2000 report from the Institute of Medicine emphasizes the range of factors that influence health using the metaphor of “Neurons to Neighborhoods” to express this idea.

Page 22

  1. This widely quoted paper and diagram, often referred to as the “Fisher-Owens Diagram” again emphasizes the factors that contribute to oral health that range from:
  2. • the mouth of children with its microflora to hard and soft tissues to the impact of substrate (diet),
  3. • Influences at the level of the child,
  4. • Influences at the level of the family, and
  5. • Influences at the level of the community
  6. The common message is that if we focus only on teeth or oral structures, we are unlikely to succeed in producing a population with good oral health. This idea raises awareness about the many other ways that those interested in oral health can intervene.

Page 23

  1. Another important concept to help understand why the U.S. health care systems work the way they do is this idea of “Commercial Determinants of Heath.” This idea is now being expressed as a component of Social Determinants of Health by the World Health Organization (WHO) and others. The WHO describes this idea as “Commercial determinants of health are a key social determinant, and refer to the conditions, actions and omissions by commercial actors that affect health.”

  1. Some key points about commercial determinants of health are listed on this slide. They include examples of ways that private sector actions affect people’s health.
  2. The United States is impacted to a greater degree by commercial determinants of health than other developed countries because our health systems are based on an entrepreneurial model and managed by and influenced by business interests to a greater degree than the health systems in other developed countries.
